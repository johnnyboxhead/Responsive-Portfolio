About Me

I'm a fun loving and hard working guy from Richmond, VA. I was born and lived in the Hopewell/Prince George area of Virginia for my first 20 years of life on this planet. I lived in and attended school in Hopewell, which is a small independent incorporated city in central Virginia. I played sports and from time to time excelled in school but didn't achieve anything spectacular, even in a small community. One huge part of my upbringing was spending time on my Grandmother's/Great Uncle's farm in Prince George where I spent the summers baling hay, fixing fences, and whatever other hard labor I could be subjected to. Those times helped create my strong work ethic, fostered a love of animals, and gave me a deep appreciation for the subtle beauty of nature; things that I carry with me to this day.


I started my professional career in 1998 with Capital One, at the time a small but promising credit card company that had recently spun off from Signet Bank. The job and hours were less than ideal but it was a way for me to get my foot in the door. I started off taking phone calls and did so for the next eight-ish years, getting several promotions and moving to different departments along the way. In 2004 I decided to make a change at Capital One and moved to our Bank, which was a small startup at the time. Once in the Bank I started to progress and now 14 years later I'm leading a team that supports our Bank contact centers in trying to help customers with our self-service offerings.


At times I'm still shocked that an unremarkable guy from a no-name town has been able to achieve what I have. I work for a Fortune 100 Company (as of 2017) and have driven initiatives that have somehow touched the lives of millions of our customers. I'm very appreciatiave for all the opportunities I've received and try to always stay humble, realizing that a huge part of my success is due to people who have believed in and helped me along the way. I look towards the future with a clear heart and an open mind and hope that the second half of my career is as surprising and fun as what I've expereinced so far.
